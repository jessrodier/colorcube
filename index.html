What is this?

ColorCube taskes in a list of colors and returns a bunch of information about the accessibility (a11y) of those colors. The first colukmn is the color itself. The second column is the legibility ratio with white, then with black. The last column is the most readable pairing given the same list of colors, and what the resulting ratio of those colors may be.

In all cases, red denotes a color contrast ratio that is too small. Green denotes a good color contrast ratio, and yellow means it is right on the edge of legibility, so be careful.

The Target Ratios:

WCAG 2.0 level AA requires a contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text. Level AAA requires a contrast ratio of 7:1 for normal text and 4.5:1 for large text. Large text is defined as 14 point (typically 18.66px) and bold or larger, or 18 point (typically 24px) or larger.

Check your hover text color vs. your body text color:

If links are not underlined, there must be 3:1 contrast between link text and body text and also an additional change (e.g., underlining) on mouse hover and keyboard focus.
